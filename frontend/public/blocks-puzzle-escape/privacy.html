PRIVACY POLICY

Blocks - Puzzle Escape

Last updated: July 23, 2026

1. Who We Are and Scope

This Privacy Policy explains how the developer and operator of Blocks - Puzzle Escape (“we,” “us,” or “our”) handles information when you use the mobile game, visit its related website, or contact us for support.

It applies to the current version of Blocks - Puzzle Escape. A separate privacy notice for a new feature will supplement this policy.

2. Privacy at a Glance

  • You can play without an account or providing your name, phone number, or email address.
  • Game progress and preferences are primarily stored locally on your device.
  • The app uses Google Mobile Ads for banner, interstitial, and optional rewarded ads.
  • The current iOS version requests non-personalized ads and does not request App Tracking Transparency permission.
  • We do not sell personal information for money.

3. Information Stored Locally

The app may use local application storage to remember level progress, completed levels, stars, scores, streaks, lives, hints, coins, unlocked themes, daily-puzzle status, and sound, music, vibration, accessibility, theme, and language preferences. This information normally remains on your device and is not sent to us.

Removing the app, clearing its storage, or changing devices may permanently remove local progress unless a platform backup restores it.

4. Advertising and Technical Data

When ads are requested or displayed, Google Mobile Ads and the operating system may automatically process limited device and usage information. Depending on your device, region, consent choices, and Google settings, this may include:

  • IP address, which may estimate a general or coarse location
  • Device, advertising, or app-instance identifiers
  • Device model, operating system, language, app version, and network information
  • Ad impressions, clicks, rewarded-ad completion, product-interaction, and advertising data
  • Crash logs, diagnostics, performance data, and fraud-prevention signals

Advertising partners process this information under their own privacy policies. We do not receive your complete advertising profile or direct identifiers such as your name from the ad SDK.

5. Information You Provide

If you contact us, we receive the information you choose to include, such as your email address, message, screenshots, device details, or support history. Please do not send sensitive information that is not needed to resolve your request.

The current version does not require an account or an in-app purchase. If purchases are offered later, Apple or Google will process payment details. We may receive transaction identifiers and entitlement status, but not full card or bank details.

6. Information We Do Not Request for Gameplay

  • Precise GPS location
  • Contacts, call logs, or messages
  • Photos, videos, microphone recordings, or camera access
  • Health, biometric, financial-account, or government-identifier data
  • Browsing or search history outside the app

7. How and Why We Use Information

  • Provide the game, save local progress, remember settings, and grant optional rewarded-ad benefits
  • Display and measure ads, limit repeated ads, and support aggregated reporting
  • Diagnose crashes, improve performance, secure the service, and prevent fraud or abuse
  • Answer support, privacy, and deletion requests
  • Comply with law and protect users, our rights, and the integrity of the service

Where a legal basis is required, we rely on performance of the requested service, legitimate interests in operating and securing the app, consent where required, and compliance with legal obligations.

8. Advertising, Consent, and Device Choices

Rewarded ads are optional, but an ad generally must be completed before the in-game reward is granted. Banner or interstitial ads may appear during normal use.

The current iOS version requests non-personalized ads and does not request permission to track you across other companies’ apps and websites. Non-personalized ads may still use contextual information, frequency capping, aggregated reporting, security signals, and limited device identifiers.

You can review advertising, privacy, and identifier controls in your iOS or Android settings. Depending on your region, Google may also show a consent or privacy-options form.

9. When Information May Be Shared

Information may be shared only as needed with advertising and technical providers such as Google Mobile Ads; Apple App Store, Google Play, hosting, and infrastructure providers; advisers or authorities when required by law; or a successor in a merger, acquisition, financing, or transfer subject to appropriate safeguards.

We do not sell personal information for money. Certain advertising disclosures may be treated as “sharing” under some privacy laws even when no money is exchanged.

10. Website Data and Cookies

The website’s hosting and analytics infrastructure may receive standard technical logs such as IP address, browser and device type, pages viewed, referral source, timestamps, and error information. Cookies or similar technologies may be used for security, basic functionality, traffic measurement, and performance.

You can restrict cookies in your browser settings, although some site functions may not work as expected.

11. Retention and Deletion

  • Local game data remains until you clear storage, reset progress, or uninstall the app.
  • Support correspondence is kept only as reasonably necessary to resolve requests, maintain records, prevent abuse, and meet legal obligations.
  • Advertising, diagnostic, and platform data is retained by each provider under its own rules.
  • Verified privacy or deletion requests are normally handled within 30 days unless law allows or requires more time.

12. Security and International Transfers

We use reasonable administrative and technical measures designed to protect information, but no method of storage or transmission is completely secure.

Providers may process information outside your country. Where required, transfers are supported by recognized safeguards, contractual protections, or another lawful mechanism.

13. Children’s Privacy

Blocks - Puzzle Escape is a general-audience puzzle game and is not specifically directed to children under 13. It does not require a child to create an account or provide a name or email address.

A parent or guardian who believes a child provided personal information to us may contact us so we can review and delete it where appropriate. Age thresholds may differ by country.

Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, restrict, object to, or receive a portable copy of personal information; withdraw consent; and complain to a local data-protection authority.

  • Manage advertising and privacy controls in device settings or any consent form shown in the app.
  • Delete local game data by clearing storage or uninstalling the app.
  • Use our deletion page or email us to submit a verified request.
  • California residents may also have rights to know, correct, delete, or opt out of sale or sharing where applicable, without discriminatory treatment.

15. Third-Party Privacy Information

Third-party services control their own practices. Review the Google Privacy Policy, Google advertising information, and Apple Privacy Policy.
